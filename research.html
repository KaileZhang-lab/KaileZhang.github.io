Soil-microbiome-plant interactions under sustainable management

These studies aim to elucidate how sustainable management in managed ecosystems shapes the links among soil health, soil microbiomes, plant health, and environmental sustainability. In agroecosystems, we evaluate multiple sustainability-oriented practices, including organic amendments (e.g., biochar), diversified crop rotations, intercropping, cover cropping, biofertilizers (e.g., synthetic microbial communities), and organic management. Collectively, this work seeks to identify microbiome-informed strategies that enhance soil function and productivity while advancing climate-resilient agroecosystems.

Papers: Netto-Ferreira et al. 2026, Luo et al. 2025, Erhunmwunse et al. 2023
